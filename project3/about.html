Project 3 Proposal

Title: QUIKKSTEP

Concept: An auto-runner styled game where the player character runs from side-to-side across the horizontal plane, bouncing back in the opposite direction when they reach the opposite end of the screen. The player has obstacles they are meant to dodge sent at them in the opposing direction, and the player is meant to dodge them by "quickstepping" (snapping up or down to another preset y-level) out of the way. It's a high score oriented game, with a scaling difficulty method of throwing more projectiles at the player over time, but with increasing the score rewarded as it gets more difficult.

Genres: Endless Runner, Action

Platform: Desktop Web

Story: No real story, kinda just abstract

Aesthetics: Unsure yet, but maybe something super minimal and abstract and dystopian, maybe like The Cube movie from 1997 but way simpler. Just spitballing though

Gameplay: The player will have no control over their horizontal movement, but will be able to control themselves on the vertical axis by snapping themselves to one of a set of predefined y-axes.

Development progress: Final

  • I wasn't thinking of WonderSwan, I was just thinking of the Scale2x filter, lol. Made some new sprites and filtered them through that. Also made some SFX using jsfxr and got a nice royalty free chiptune loop playing in the background.
  • I implemented a prevention measure for later intensities and the possibility of obstacles spawning on top of you. At all points in the game, there's a threshold at the edges of the screen where obstacle spawning is disabled to prevent unfair moments where something spawns directly on top of you with no way to react. As the intensity increases and the player speeds up, this threshold will get incrementally larger to address the fact that the player may get fast enough to surpass the threshold before the spawn check goes through, causing unfair spawns to occur anyways. In extreme edgecases, at ludicrously high intensities the thresholds will overlap and cover the entire playspace, but at that point the player would be already pinging back and forth between existing obstacles with inhuman reaction times, so I don't see a problem with it.
  • I also implemented some measures to prevent legitimate loss-states that just feel unfair, such as trying to dodge an obstacle but being just too late and dying as the very fringes of the sprites connect, or trying to weave between obstacles and hitting one by accident. These may be techincally deserved failures, but they feel terrible. I added both an invulnerability state for when the player moves, giving them 8 frames of invulnerability for a bit of leniency when it comes to situations like weaving, as well as a collision timer. With this timer, the player does not instantly lose upon impact with an obstacle; rather, a 5 frame timer starts, and if 5 frames pass and collision is still occurring, THEN the player dies. This is for scenarios such as avoiding obstacles by the skin of your teeth, since you did technically connect, and you should have lost, but that wouldn't have felt fair, so you get a pass.
  • The addition of these invuln states did open up the door for an exploit, where endlessly spamming up and down would have given the player infinite invulnerability, negating all challenge, so I had to implement some logic to try and counter this. It isn't foolproof and it may not function entirely as imagined, but to me it feels like it works well enough.
  • Finally, I updated the CSS to match how the new sprites look better, as well as adding more explicit explanations of how the game works alongside proper credits.
